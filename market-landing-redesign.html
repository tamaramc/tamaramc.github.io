Boston Public Market Landing Page Concept

Team: Dani, Tamara, Obie, Chris, Jackie, Jamie

This project was the result of a 24 hour Hackathon with the Boston Public Market and the StartUp Institue Boston.

Problem
  • How can the market use technology to better inform the public about its mission and offerings?
Hypothesis
  • The website could be redesigned to better tell the market's story, leverage existing promotional materials, and drive customer participation with the website.
Discovery
  • Observed and interviewed customers and vendors at the market.
  • Shopped at the market on three occasions.
  • Researched data on site visits and market's use of social media.
  • Conducted user research on existing site.
  • Completed competitive analysis.
  • Existing site. The entire first screen is filled with Twitter feeds and a confusing "View More" call to action.
Insights

Most people came to the market for one of four reasons:

  • For a quick and healthy lunch near their workplace
  • As a special outing or day trip
  • To purchase from a specific vendor
  • They discovered the market by chance while walking by

Customers wanted to find the most basic information on the site.

  • What was being sold
  • Where vendors were located inside the market
  • Which vendors sold prepared or grab and go food
  • Redesign mock-up from the Hackathon
Design Process

During the Hackathon, I put together a quick mockup in Sketch of a redesign for the landing page. The look and feel of the site, colors, and fonts are based on existing promotional print materials. I added:

  • Search function
  • Banner image to draw in visitors with clear text explaining the local mission of the market
  • Featured vendor video section. A goal of the market was to make the experience more personal.
  • Link for visitors to access social media
  • List of products sold
  • Kitchen-themed background to match the feel of the market's community space
  • Redesigned wireframe

Wireframes

The Hackathon didn't give us enough time to fully explore the UI of the site.

After the Hackathon, I went back to user interviews and competitive analysis to better inform my design decisions on the layout.

I focused on the following customer goals:

  • Discovering lunch options quickly
  • Finding vendor locations quickly
  • Finding basic information such as location, parking, hours
  • Learning what is sold and by whom
  • Finding market events easily
