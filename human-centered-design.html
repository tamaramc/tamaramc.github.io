IDEO: Insights to Innovation

Team: Tamara, Alexia, Carla, Tara, Cornelia

In February 2016 I lead team Bos Innovation in Ideo's Insights to Innovation Course. The course brought together a global community to explore human-centered design principles through real life exercises. Our team collaborated via Skype across four time zones.

Challenge
  • How might we gain insights into modern 70 year olds?
Observation

Our first task was to simply observe 70 year olds. I observed in public places such as the grocery store, libary, and train. One woman I observed modified an old umbrella to create a lightweight, collapsible walking stick.

Interviews

Each of our team members interviewed 70 year olds in their communities. We compared notes across cultures to tease out themes. Our team represented three countries while the greater online community in the course included people from all over the world.

  • As an empathy exercise I limited my use of technology to the computers at the library.
Empathy Exercises

We wanted to empathize as much as possible with the 70 year olds we met.

  • I limited my technology use to the computers at the library for a day.
  • I lived on a minimal food budget for a week to better understand the challenges faced by seniors living on fixed incomes.

View empathy pdf
  • We used sticky notes and the online brainstorming tool Storm Board to find themes.
Crafting Insights

We brought together our observations to find common themes and to identify key insights.

  • With freedom from obligation, retired seniors find purpose through giving back.
  • Striving forward through thoughtful growth. Seniors want to continue learning and growing as individuals.
  • Technology for connecting with family, not for sharing with the world. For the most part seniors valued technology as a tool to communicate with their kids, but sharing on the web didn't match their values.
  • Aging body, but maintaining vitality. Seniors did not want to be labeled "old". Maintaining vitality was important to them, both to stay healthy and to help their families.
