Food Truck Mobile Application

Team: Dani, Tamara, Frank, Jean, Rich

This application design arose from a half day design sprint led by Tom Hughes at the Startup Institute.

Problem
  • How might we make the ordering process faster and easier for food truck customers?
Hypothesis
  • Food truck customers would find value in pre-ordering their food online.
Discovery
  • Conducted interviews with 36 food truck customers.
  • Over 70% responded positively when asked if they would use an online ordering app for food trucks.
  • Time-savings was the number one reason customers saw value in the app.
  • Target audience identified as workers in need of a quick meal.
Design Process

I initially sketched out a potential customer flow on paper from onboarding to purchase. Time was the biggest motivator for our target customer. I tried to make the interface as simple as possible.

One question that came up is whether the customer would value the geolocation aspect of the app or simply rely on saved favorites. A downtown worker might quickly learn which trucks are available on regular basis and not need the map function.

Prototyping

I created visual elements in Illustrator and Sketch to bring the idea to life, then made a prototype in Keynote. Keynote can be used as a quick tool to simulate animations.

Iteration and Validation

The next step in the design process would be to test out the prototype, identify areas not working and iterate based on that testing.

A minimal viable product might only provide online menus and ordering rather than geolocation features. It might also focus on trucks in a small geographic range such as around Boston's South Station.
